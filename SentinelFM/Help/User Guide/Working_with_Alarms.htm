Working with Alarms

You can set sensors that are attached to different parts of a vehicle to trigger alarms when activated. There are three levels of alarms: No Alarm, Warning, and Critical Alarm. For details, see Modifying Sensor Names and Alarm Severity.

 

Warnings or critical alarms will be generated for a vehicle only if the box in the vehicle is armed. For details, see Sensors, Outputs and Commands.

 

If a critical alarm is triggered for a vehicle, users who have been set up will receive notification by email. Any device that can receive messages in standard email format such as alphanumeric pagers, SMS-capable cell phones, etc. can be contacted. For details, see Setting Up Email Addresses for Alarm Notification.

 

Whenever an alarm is triggered, you can check for up-to-date information about it in the Alarms screen, the scrolling list at the top right of the Map screen. Two levels of alarms are displayed: warnings and critical alarms. The list shows all alarms that have been activated within the last 24 hours, with the most recent one at the top of the list.

You can view more information about an alarm, accept it or close it.

Viewing Detailed Information About an Alarm

To view information about an alarm:

 

  1. In the Alarms scrolling window, move the mouse over the alarm that you want to work with. The list stops scrolling.

  2. Click to open the Alarm Info screen. View details about the selected alarm. Note the following fields:

    • Alarm Description: the name of the sensor that was triggered and the action that triggered it (for example, "trunk:open")

    • Alarm State: either "new" or "accepted"

    • Alarm Severity: either "critical" or "warning"

    • Street Address: the address or landmark closest to where the alarm occurred

  1. To close the Alarm Info screen, click Close.

Accepting or Closing an Alarm

Accepting an alarm causes the vehicle that triggered the alarm to move to the top of the list in the vehicle grid. Its current GPS position is displayed as an icon on the Map at street-level detail.

 

In the Alarms screen, the alarm changes to green. It remains on the Alarms screen for 24 hours, unless it is closed first.

 

Closing an alarm removes it permanently from the Alarms screen.

 

To accept or close an alarm:

 

  1. In the Alarms scrolling window, move the mouse over the alarm that you want to work with. The list stops scrolling.

  2. Click to open the Alarm Info screen.

    • To accept the alarm, click Accept Alarm. The alarm text in the Alarms screen changes to green.

    • To close the alarm, click Close Alarm. The alarm text is removed from the Alarms screen.

 

During a single incident with a particular vehicle the system may have generated several alarms related to the incident.  The User has the option, when viewing the Alarm Info screen related to a particular alarm, to close all open alarms for that vehicle by selecting the Close All Vehicle Alarms button.  The User is asked to please click the button again to confirm.  Once confirmed, all open alarms for that particular vehicle are removed from the scrolling Alarms window.
