Centering, Zooming, and other Map Functions

To use the various mapping functions available in the Sentinel-FM system, in the Map screen, a User must generally first display the vehicles they want to work with. For details, see Displaying Vehicles in the Map Screen.  A User can then use the following procedures to manage the map display.

Re-centering the Map on a Selected Point

To center the map on a point where you click with the left mouse button:

  1. On the left side of the map, enable the re-center button   (This is the default option - button text turns yellow when enabled.)

  1. Click anywhere on the map.

  2. The point where you clicked becomes the center of a new map display.

 

 

Re-centering the Map on a Vehicle or Vehicles

 

When you first display a vehicle on the map, it is centered. Sometimes as you navigate through the map, the map may move so that you are no longer able to view the vehicle(s) you are monitoring. To re-center the map on a particular vehicle or vehicles:

  1. Ensure vehicle(s) are being displayed on the Map screen. For details, see Displaying Vehicles on the Map Screen.

  2. While you are working with the standard Map screen, if the vehicle(s) are no longer visible, click the Map It button.  All selected vehicles will appear centered on the updated map display.

  3. If you are working with the Full Screen Map display, if the vehicle(s) are no longer visible, click on the Show All button .  All selected vehicles will appear centered on the updated map display.

 

Zooming

There are several ways to zoom in or out on the map.  These include rectangle zoom, using the zoom-in and zoom-out buttons, using the right click menu or using the pre-defined zoom buttons.

Zooming In Using Rectangle Zoom

With the rectangle zoom function, you outline an area, and the map zooms in on it.

 

To use the rectangle zoom:

 

  1. Click on one corner of the area you want to zoom in on.

  2. Keeping the mouse button held down drag the mouse to the opposite corner of the area. As you drag the mouse, a dashed box appears over the map.
     


 

  1. Release the mouse button. The map zooms in on the area within the dashed box.

Zooming In or Out Using the Zoom Buttons



 

Use the zoom buttons on the left side of the map to set whether you want to zoom in  or zoom out when you click on a map location with the left mouse button. Once you choose one of these buttons the button text turns yellow indicating it is enabled and it remains enabled until you choose a different one.

 

To use the zoom buttons:

 

  1. Enable either the zoom in or the zoom out button by clicking on it.

  2. Click anywhere on the map.

  3. The map zooms in or out on the point where you clicked. The point where you clicked will be the center of the new map display.

  4. You can keep clicking on the map and it will continue to zoom in or out, depending on which button you enabled.

 

 

Zooming In or Out Using the Right Click Mouse Menu

 

At any time a User can use the right click mouse menu to zoom in or zoom out on the current center of the map display.

 

 

Zooming In or Out Using Predefined Zoom Levels

The vertical zoom bar on the upper left side of the map contains predefined zoom level buttons. These buttons zoom in or zoom out at preset levels on the center of the current map display, so you do not need to click on the map.

 

The numbered buttons zoom to six predefined levels. The plus and minus buttons zoom to a larger number of levels.

  • To zoom to one of the six predefined zoom levels, click one of the numbered buttons .

  • To zoom in or out one zoom level at a time, click the plus button

or the minus button .

 

Note: These buttons override the zoom in and zoom out buttons at the left of the map.

 

Other Map Functions

Determining the Current Zoom Level of the Map

To determine the current zoom level of the map, in the vertical zoom bar on the upper left side of the map, identify the button whose text is highlighted in yellow.

 

Scrolling the Map North, South, East or West

To scroll the map in any of the four cardinal directions, click one of the arrows on the edges of the map  .

 

Viewing the Map Display Full Screen

To view the Map more easily, click Full Screen Map. The map is displayed, along with all of its standard functions, in its own full screen window. Alarms appear as moving text at the bottom of the screen. The vehicle grid does not appear; to view current information for a vehicle, move the mouse over the vehicle icon on the map, and a popup window appears. To return to the standard Map screen, click Close Window.

 

Printing the Currently Displayed Map

To print the current map display, right-click on the map and choose Print Map. A standard Print dialog appears where you can choose settings before you print.
