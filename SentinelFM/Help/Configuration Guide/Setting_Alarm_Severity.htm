Modifying Input Sensor Alarm Severity

 

Note: Warnings or critical alarms will be generated for a vehicle only if the box in the vehicle is armed. For more information about sensors, as well as details about arming the box, see Viewing Sensors and Sending Outputs or Commands.

Alarm Severity Levels

You can decide whether a particular physical or virtual sensor should trigger an alarm by assigning it a severity. For all levels of alarm, sensors that were triggered are reported in the History screen. (See Viewing Vehicle Path History.) You can also create a report that lists when these sensors were triggered. (See Report Descriptions.)

 

There are several levels of severity for alarms:

 

  • No Alarm -- No alarm appears in the Alarms window in the Map screen; no email is sent out.

  • Notify -- No alarm appears in the Alarms window in the Map screen. People who were entered in the email list for alarm notification for that fleet or that specific vehicle receive an email.

  • Warning -- Appears in gold in the Alarms screen in the Map screen, thus notifying the dispatcher. People who were entered in the email list for alarm notification for that fleet or that specific vehicle also receive an email.

  • Critical -- Appears in red in the Alarms screen in the Map screen, thus notifying the dispatcher. People who were entered in the email list for alarm notification for that fleet or that specific vehicle also receive an email.

 

In an example of physical sensors and alarms, the "Action Off" state for a "Door" sensor would usually be "Closed", while the "Action On" state would be "Open". However, having a car door open may not be considered severe enough to trigger an alarm, so you could assign it a severity of "No Alarm".

 

For a "Panic Button" physical sensor, however, when you specify that the "Action On" state is "On", you may consider this serious enough to assign it a severity of "Critical".  The virtual sensor panic button feature on the key fob (pressing two buttons at the same time) would also likely be assigned an alarm severity of "Critical".

  1. From any Sentinel-FM page, click the Configuration hyperlink on the top of the screen. On the Vehicles tab, choose the Sensors/Alarms/Messages sub tab, and choose a fleet, then a vehicle. Wait for the list of sensors to appear.
